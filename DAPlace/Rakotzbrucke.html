Rakotzbrücke, also called the Devil's Bridge, looks like something that belongs in a fairytale.

This unique looking bridge is tucked away in a small park in Saxony, Germany. Getting here can be a challenge, since Rakotzbrücke is not located near any major city. However, if you combine a trip here with other nearby destinations, you can turn this visit into a wonderful day trip.

IMPORTANT UPDATE:

The bridge recently underwent a renovation project is completed. Our photos are from our visit in September 2017.

Rakotzbrücke was built in 1860 by human hands. The entire bridge is manmade, including the jagged, rocky spires on either end of the bridge and the pointed rocks that sit out in the lake. Even though Satan did not play a role in the construction of this bridge, it still gets the name Devil’s Bridge for its delicate arch. It's so extraordinary that Satan must have built it..
